]]> Human errors can ruin the whole concept and scenario of any design. When it comes to website designing, the situation is even more critical. When a website or illustration on a website is viewed over a big screen, whether you want it or not human errors can come under limelight automatically.

Nowadays most websites are designed over any specified theme. This trend makes the job of designers even more difficult. One single error can be a cause of rejection of design from your clients because with the very little mistake the whole theme get disturbed.

Being a human we cannot avoid errors and there is no harm in making errors until they become a cause of rejection of your design. Here are some tips through which one can easily find human errors in a website design.

ALSO READ: 6 App Analytics Strategies That You Need To Keep an Eye On

Zoom In

When you create a mock-up or before presenting the idea to your client, check every detail by zooming in and zooming out the design again and again. Zooming option allows you to view every minor and major detail in your design properly. This cross-check will help you to sort out things before client`s eye can catch them.

Colors in Number

Depending upon the quality of monitor/screen and technology that a designer is using the display of colors can vary. Sometimes the difference is of high level that it can cause major issues for any design. They can affect the aesthetic appeal and disturb the theme. To avoid such issues it is suggested to add colors by noticing the numerical figure assigned for every tone of color in software or shade card.

Grids

Even if you are making a grid-free design for the website, you have to follow certain scales to make your design effective, appealing and organized. Following grinds or aligning the design elements with reference to lines can be helpful to detect orientation and placement errors in any design.

Take reviews

Showing your design to multiple people and allowing them to criticize openly is a good practice. Firstly, it will help you to improve. Secondly, your design navigation will be tested practically. Thirdly, you can learn psychological tactics to deal people with designing. Fourthly, you will be learning to counter errors or concepts of your design.

Test before Going Live

 

Test the design, interface, navigation, color scheme and functionality of website on several devices to make sure that it is working properly and everything is just as accurate as you wished them to be. Test them on different IOS,  as well as on cell phones, tablets, PCs, Gaming PCs, Smart TVs etc. because sometimes varying the view from different devices can be helpful to find out your design errors.

Hopefully, you find my blog knowledgeable and helpful. Follow mentioned techniques to increase the proficiency and accuracy of your design so that you can learn new things and can present best in front of your clients.

]]> It is a general observation that the main focus of most B2B businesses is more inclined towards generating and converting leads, and therefore, they tend to ignore the potential of a B2B-optimzied web design for their business. A majority of them are of the view that a plain website that has all of their products and services listed is enough to maintain their presence in the cyber world.

However, they couldn’t be more wrong.

Web design plays a significant role for B2B and B2C businesses alike. While the B2C business industry has long realized the significance of action-oriented site design and reaping maximum benefits out of it, B2B businesses are clearly leaving a lot of potential on the table.

Here’s why a B2B centric web design is important for your business.

ALSO READ: Google Search Console – Is the New Update Worth It?

§  Branding

Your website is the personification of your business in the cyber sphere, and people – including your customers, leads as well as your competitors – will turn to it whenever they are looking for you online. It is your digital shop front and reflects the quality, credibility and professionalism of your business. A B2B centric web design will optimize your website for on-point branding in the online market.

§  Conversion

The purpose of a website is to provide information about your business and attract more visitors. But more than that, it should also be able to convert your leads. Therefore, for successful conversions, your website design should be such that it compels visitors to make a purchase decision. This can easily be done by incorporating creative and informative content, simplistic navigation, and strong CTAs. If your site design is not sales-oriented, it wouldn’t facilitate online sales – no matter you are a B2B or B2C enterprise.

§  Search Engine Ranking

A B2B optimized website design will enhance your online visibility and exposure among your online audience by improving your search engine ranking. B2B centric web designs feature custom images, unique content, convenient navigation and a user-friendly interface – and that’s what Google encourages and rewards with better rankings. In addition to that, a blog to complement your website, that’s regularly updated will do wonders in improving your search engine ranking and will help you garner maximum social media response.

§  Accessibility

Mobile responsiveness and cross-platform compatibility has become essential for a website to perform at its best and attract maximum traffic. Your customers, leads, competitors and everyone else looking for your business online refer to your site from different sources. While a majority of online searches today are being made through mobiles and handheld devices, your website should still be optimized to be compatible with different desktop and mobile devices.

 

 

Here’s how you can optimize your website to make it more B2B oriented:

  • Know your target audience
  • Use high-quality custom graphics
  • Add a human touch
  • Facilitate engagement

]]> Typography is undoubtedly one of the most prestigious yet complex disciplines in graphic design. It doesn’t matter how skilled you are in this specific domain of graphic design, there’s something always to learn. The weird part about typography is that it isn’t constant from any angle. There are several different fonts, hundreds of different combination and thousands of different concepts that fall under the umbrella of typography.

Our team of creative thinkers love to share their ideas here at the The Web Founders blog to help young design talent in becoming better every passing day. Here are a few tips and tricks to keep your creative juices flowing.

Invest Time

Keep one thing in mind: good typography, takes time. Invest as much time as you can to create something incomparable. Search for a typeface that suits your idea, background, color, shapes in the picture and much more. Starting with basic fonts can be easier but continuing to work only with the basics isn’t the right route to the future. Keep your research strong and invest your time properly on your typography for excellent results.

Read Also: Design Brief – Things You Need To Focus On

Limit Your Typefaces

Yes, you need a combination of different fonts but you need to limit your typefaces to a maximum of 2 or 3. Anything more than 5 fonts per design is just going to take the essence away from the design. Use fewer designs with a bigger impact on the design to create results that actually matter for your business.

Trends Can Be Deceiving

Futureproofing a superior design is important but falling victim to trends just might be a huge mistake. Trends can be deceiving! It doesn’t matter if the latest designs in the industry use Gotham or Trebuchet, if they don’t go with your brand and your design you don’t have to use it. Pay attention to the design and make sure it’s as perfect as can be.
