]]> Google ranking is the fluctuating position of your website in the search engine result page which determines the success and failure of your website. It is not always possible to retain a stable ranking position for a longer time as it always fluctuates upward and downward.

It is necessary for you to have a nicely built search engine friendly website by complying with proper search marketing strategies to help your website retain the 1st-page ranking.

In this blog post, we will discuss the 8 things which do not directly affect your Google ranking.

ALSO READ: Boost Your Business Revenue With These 3 Steps

Website age

Website age is an essential search engine optimization factor which does not directly affect your ranking.  The older age registration of your website domain does provide a reliable source of authority and trust for Google. However, the necessary things to do are quality relevant backlink profile which increases the value of the website with original quality content which drives the attention of readers.

Social sharing

Social sharing links also do not affect your Google ranking. As Google keeps no concerns with your increasing number of shares, likes, and tweet counts. However, social sharing does help in boosting valuable traffic for your website. Social sharing helps in building a strong brand portfolio for your website to reach more audience with an increase in reputation.

Bounce rate

Bounce rate is the stepping back of the visitor from your website. It spoils the reputation of your website in Google. The higher bounce rate leads to a lower click-through rate with a decrease in website traffic. The good or bad bounce rate does not affect your website ranking. It is suitable for your website to sustain visitors by producing quality engaging content with more dwell time.

Heading tags

The heading tags are HTML tags which comprise of six varieties of tags ranging from h1 to h6. The <h1> is the most substantial heading tag and <h6> is the smallest heading tags in your website. These tags do not affect your website ranking but are necessary for showing the website status.

Robots.txt and XML sitemap

The robots.txt is Meta source file which has a purpose of restricting the bots to crawl the specific pages in your website. XML sitemap provides the fast crawling and indexing of your website pages to let know Google about them. These files do not affect your ranking of the website but are essential factors for SEO.

Meta keywords

Meta keywords are the generic list of HTML keywords which help to show up ranking for your website. The use of these Meta keywords is not much recommended in modern SEO, and they also do not much affect your website ranking.

Alt Image Keywords

The purpose of the alt image tag is to provide ranking by labeling a keyword title to your website images. The alt image keywords also do not affect much to your website ranking.

Schema markup

Schema markup is an ideal technique to boost up enhanced and dynamic search listing for your website. It better works for local businesses, book reviews, movies, and restaurants. The use of schema markup also does not affect your website ranking.

]]> Created website by putting all efforts, without taking much care about website’s metrics, is just like walking down a dark cave and assuming someone will torch a light for you.

Metrics are numbers that state accurate measurement and provide a base to suggest enhancements. Considering one measurement is not enough to measure the effectiveness of your website. Both operational and financial metrics are commonly used to track and assess the status of a specific process.

Most of the professional web designers find difficult to choose right metric. For convenience, I’ve compiled some of the well-known metrics helpful for your website:

ALSO READ: How to Market Your Self on Social Media in 6 Steps

1.    Number of Website Visitors

It is crucial to possess reliable source to track incoming traffic on your custom website. For the respective purpose, there’re three primary sources or three type of website visitors. The one who enters directly into your site by typing URL in browser address. The one who enters your site based on the search request. The last ones are the referral visitors. All three sources have a different level of conversion, and you should calculate traffic of each.

2.    Bounce Rate

In internet marketing, bounce rate refers to the measurement of people entering your website without going to any other webpage. Simply put, it is just like a single-page session that generates only a single request to the analytics server. Low bounce rate means that visitors’ engagement is good on your site. On the contrary, high bounce rate is an indicator of a bad situation.

3.    Exit Pages

Most of the visitors often get confused between “bounce” and “exit.”  Both are different metrics to measure. Unlike a “bounce,” when a site visitors enter to your site and hardly views one page, an “exit” is when a user enters and visits a number of pages and then abandon your site. If your website has a high exit rate, it has some problem. Knowing the exit rates and reasons one can improve site traffic and so conversion.

4.    Crawl Errors

Besides the metrics mentioned above, Google Search Console displays you crawl errors. It is pointless to say that, any crawl error on the website should be addressed soon, having crawl errors on site is as just your website does not work as they are supposed to and doesn’t bring you any conversion. It is imperative to check crawl errors report once a week and deal with what you find. High crawl errors mean that your site is either down or misconfigured. Low crawl errors is a temporary condition and also indicate your improper site configuration.

5.    Final Conversions

To know how well your strategy is working you need to know about final conversions. It is a metric and very simple to achieve. It is an achievement of the end goal you set in front of your business. Final conversion is the only metric that allows you if you should change anything on your website.

Though there are numerous other subtle metrics, but metrics mentioned above, are enough to make or destroy your professional website.

]]> In today’s world it is no longer a wonder that more than 70% of businesses possess online existence in the face of websites. Unfortunately, only few of them know the importance of making a website search engine friendly.

What does SEO friendly website really mean? Simply it is a website that is easy to read and understand by the search engines. SEO friendly website is actually a visitor friendly website.

Some of the important characteristics of SEO friendly websites are:

  • To be responsive
  • To be not too fussy or ornate to read
  • Should be User-friendly
  • Should be mobile-responsive
  • Should possess optimized images with the text
  • Having clear hierarchy and text-based Navigation Structure

ALSO READ: 2 Ways Voice Search Turn Your SEO on its Brain

  1. Responsiveness

Responsiveness is a key to make your web page look appealing on all browsers and devices- desktop, mobile phone, and, tablet. Responsive design has three principles to be executed to work properly: Fluid grids, Media queries, and flexible images. To be responsive actually means to be adaptable.

  1. Optimizing Images

Another way to make your site SEO friendly is to optimize images with texts. Just name your images descriptively, optimize your alt attributes, choose image dimensions, optimize thumbnails, use image sitemaps and test your images.

In fact, there’re many compression tools available that make the art of image optimization possible. ImageRecycle, OptimiZilla, Tinypng, giftofspeed, and kraken are some of the examples to compress multiple images or files in one go.

  1. Videos and Infographics

Info graphics are  a visual representation of data beneficial to the website. Because more than 85% of information transmitted to brain is visual.

The act of embedding videos to the website is best way to get your message across. By putting interactive and interesting videos you can get heavy traffic on your site. As 44% of Google search results feature video.

  1. Great UX Leads to High Ranking

Users are prior and good user experience help your site to convert more. If users coming your site find any distractions and difficulty, they’ll abandon. On the other hand, site having appealing look, intuitiveness, easy to navigate with clear instructions get more chance to engage users.

  1. Make your URL’s User Friendly

It is not only your responsive web layout or good content but also URL that should be user friendly. It is easy to read and understand. User friendly URL help people to remember the web address, increase click through rate and even influence social metrics.

Just keep it short, use relevant keywords, use real words, be descriptive, and not to prefer using tracking parameters.

At the end, these five elements are enough to make your site SEO friendly. But there are many other things you can do, like:

  • Incorporating social media elements into your website
  • Add 2-5 relevant keywords to the single web page
  • Use JavaScript in restricted manner, do not use it for whole site design
  • Do not use too much flashy elements, it can be distracting
  • Create page hierarchy properly
  • Write unique titles and meta descriptions
  • Reduce Code bloat, HTML table Usage
  • Lessen the number of on-page links
  • Implement proper heading tag and structured data
  • Use properly Coded lists
  • Keep an eye on your security certificate renewals
