]]> The logo doesn’t need any introduction as a business recognition. It is a small figurative mark of your business that gives your business a ‘recognition’. Holding a recognition of business, the importance of well-designed logo cannot be neglected. History is packed with numerous famous logo designs and their success stories. If we take an example of our very own Nike’s Logo, we all know the company’s logo is famous for its simplicity and minimal approach that has taken a design to the next level. It is simple, easy to understand, easy to recall, and one of the best examples of ‘Less is more’ approach. We can find a number of designs just like Nike who are known for their unique design. Some have incorporated minimal principle, and some are the exemplification of maximalism principle. Which principle should be used depends on your business nature, however, it is important for each design to stand out and be noticeable among the array of other logo design. In fact, this is how your logo can succeed and so may your business to grab maximum eyeballs and ultimately makes more sales or increases ROI. The study reveals that your logo designs directly impacts the customer purchasing behavior. It is one of the things that helps people all across the world to understand your business nature, brand’s offerings, business value or worth. With such immense significance, one cannot say ‘NO’ to its importance. Making these small figurative marks as noticeable as much possible is the only way to get your business message out to the mass. But, that needs a lot of struggle, consideration, and witty approach. Don’t worry, I have taken some of the most important tips out from the experts for you. So, without any further ado. Go ahead:

ALSO READ: How to Solve the 5 Biggest Problems with Marketing…….

1.  Go With Plan

Always go with planning. Pre-planned things are more likely to succeed than unplanned things. Make sure you are planning each thing properly. How your logo should be, what customers will be its targeting, what message it will convey, how flexible it should be, and there are countless things need to be planned.

2.  Don’t Fall For Premade Custom Logos, Hire an Expert

That’s completely fine if you know not much about logo designing since you are an entrepreneur, not a designer. There’s always a good alternative. Hire an expert designer who knows basic to advance principles of designs, color psychology, and possesses years of experience in the reputable organization.

Commonly, many people have been seen going for a premade logos. Premade or ready-made logos are not appropriate for your business. They are not unique and so should not be used.

3.  Don’t Complicate, Make It Simple

Things if not simple, will sucks – to you and to your customers who are speculating on your logo and have lots of expectations from you. It is not good in any way to make them angry and confused. In fact, the purpose of the logo is to convey brand’s message. If they are multifaceted, they will not serve their primary goal.

4.  Avoid Unnecessariness, Incorporate Only Needed Things

This point is an extension of them –. With simplicity, it meant to incorporate only things your logo needs or you think essential to add. Take a paper and pen out. Write about your business—what your business is about, who your customers are, what they do, and all. Write five two-three lines on each and then summarize. Hand over this summary to your designer and ask him to incorporate them as much as possible. Heavy ornaments, loud color tones, excessive text are the things can make your design meager.

5.  Get Some Inspiration

The present market is overflowing with a number of good-to-inspire logo designs. Why not take some inspiration and take a hold of the good things from them? Research the market, get some good logo designs to see, study them and take inspiration for your coming business logo. By inspiration, it doesn’t mean to steal their work.

Last Thought

The logo is crucial for your business. Make it stand out and noticeable. Make your business well-known in the eyes of the public and get more sales. Directly or indirectly, it all starts with your logo, so don’t compromise with its eminence.

]]> Logo deSignagency has been changing and progressing day by day. Things that were cool or appealed people of yesterday may not be interactive for today’s folks. In this trend-changing environment, designers have to face many difficulties in creating a market-competitive design that will stay interactive for a long period.

The designer must know what concept of designing or strategy is productive to compete for the present market. For this, I’ve collected ultimate logo design trends that are being used by some of the best logo design companies in 2018.

  1. Spruce up Logo for your custom logo design company

Go with simplicity to avoid scruffiness in design. It is all about to make the logo elegant and neat by trimming all the unnecessary details and leaving only the essentials.

Logo simplification makes a huge impact as we all want simplification in things. Modern technology has made quite easy to generate a cleaner perception of the logo.

  1. Monochrome Logo

The monochrome logo is another hot trend for this year. It is made up of a single color. It is also called one-color logo. Mostly used color is black and white. Prodigital, Myphilosphy, and Toyka are some of the examples of the monochrome logo.

  1. Simple Geometrical Shapes

If you’re using complex shapes in logos, you need to check this new trend. Now trend has changed, you have to change too. Design logo with simple shapes. Start with clean lines, points, and circles then add some decent colors. Consider the designs of Nike, Fendi, Giorgio Armani, Mitsubishi, National Geographic, and Cisco logos. They could be a big inspiration for your new logo design.

  1. Letter-Stacking Logo

Creating a stacked-type logo is becoming more common today. Most of the designers are using this newest trend with contrast colors. It is used to make viewers unravel what is being said, providing them easiness to remember with creating visual hierarchy in the logo.

  1. Hand-Lettering Logo

The trend of creating Hand-lettering logo has been dominating for the last couple of years. Interestingly in 2018, the importance of this trend remains the same. In fact, it is an ultimate technique allow the designer to sketch his tails, serifs, ligatures, and loops to create an emotional connection with the audience. The hand-lettering logos of FedEx and Cocacola are one of those hand-lettering logos that are depicting a vivid brand story successfully.

  1. Add Features and Wings

Opting this trendy logo style is enough to make your logo memorable and stand out in the completive market. Add some wings or features and make your design fly high among other logos.

  1. Monogram Logos

Monogram logos are considered as an evergreen type of logo. It has been around since ancient Greece era but prospered in the late 19th century when fashion industries started using it.

Monogram Logos are made up of two or more letters to form one recognizable symbol that can depict your brand or business identity.

WRAPPING IT UP

2018 is with us, brought great things and still has many things in store for custom logo designers. I’ve revealed some of them, now it’s your turn to hunt more and choose the best way to design. You can consider these trends also to make a killer logo.

]]> Have you ever laid your eyes on a logo that’s just plain bad? I bet you have. It seems like a terrible thing to say, but it’s a fact that the increase in bad logo designs are increasing. Laymen or design professionals, no matter who you are, some logos are just too hard to like. Some people actually consider a bad logo to be a reflection of ‘brand weakness’. Believe it or not, these ‘people’ happen to be some of the best design professionals in the world.

So, if you’re a logo designer or maybe a logo design company that designed a bad logo, does that make you a bad design professional/agency? The answer is no. Even the best of us make mistakes. However, these mistakes come back to haunt you heavily when the time comes. Why wait for a career suicide when you can work on the red flags and turn them green?

In this blog post, we’ll be discussing some of the most prominent mistakes that logo designers make frequently. So without running around in circles, let’s jump right into it.

A Very Busy Logo Design

This is perhaps one of the biggest mistakes that I’ve seen over the years. In fact, I’ve seen esteemed experts in the design industry such as Jason Li, Co-Founder of Tum Yum DeSignagency discuss this problem in the past as well. You can have a look at his blog here.

A busy logo is always going to get a bad impression from the targeted audience. If there’s too much going on in the logo design, your potential custom won’t know where to pay attention. One of the many reasons why minimal design is progressing in general. There’s always one ‘sweet spot’ in your logo that really is key in creating results. When there’s too much happening in the logo, you won’t really get the chance to impress your customer. Of course, elements are important to your logo, but make sure the design in the perfect flow. Focus on not overcrowding the logo with unnecessary elements. In most cases, one single element is enough to do the trick.

Also Read: Design Brief – Things You Need To Focus On

Poor Focus on Brand Identity

This mistake is one of the most repetitive mistakes that I’ve seen happen. This even happens to the biggest of brands. As a logo designer and/or as a logo deSignagency, you need to realize that the logo isn’t solely visible on one placement. From social media display pictures to business cards, the brand identity needs to customize itself. A huge part of the brand identity is the logo design itself and that’s where things get rough.

Thorough research on the logo design placements is very important. As a design professional, you need to have a list of placements lined up to offer the client. You can also include this as a question in your design brief. Most of the times, clients aren’t well aware of brand identity. As a logo design professional, it is your duty to not only educate the client but assist them as well.

A Victim to Cliché

Now, this is one mistake that sometimes rattles up a lot of logo designers and even logo design companies. From the sparks, to the bevels and even the swooshes, there are several cliché design trends that are overused in the industry.

What confuses graphic designers around the world is that at one hand people endorse design trends and at the other, hate them in general. Well, it’s simple. There are several areas where design trends actually work. Logo Design isn’t one of them. A notable example is Material Design that is being followed by designers worldwide for app and web design. On the contrary, it can’t be used in the field of logo design. Logos define individuality and if there’s even a small similarity between two logos, the logo isn’t worth it. To avoid this mistake, research is key. Make the most out of your research and your logo design will almost always be a masterpiece with its standalone brilliance.

All in all, these are three of the biggest mistakes that are consistently invading the logo design industry. Your logo is vital for your business, regardless of your industry. Focus on your brand’s identity and it will change the way your customers react to your business in general.
